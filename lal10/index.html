Kalamkari - Tradition of painted clothes

October 28, 2014

Ever thought that the dress you wear can be outcome of a palette of a painter who invests immense creativity and energy to bring these dresses to life. Saris and suits that are hand painted with extra ordinary diligence using art forms that are preserved and nurtured for centuries are here for you to admire – The Kalamkari!! Kalamkari is a type of hand-painting on cotton textile, produced in parts of India, especially Andhra Pradesh. The word is derived from the Persian words ghalam (pen) and kari (craftsmanship), meaning drawing with a pen (Ghalamkar).
